Frequently Asked Questions

1. What Is the PPVT Test

- A brief, easy-to-use individually administered norm-referenced assessment of listening comprehension for spoken words in Standard English

- Receptive (hearing) vocabulary test

- Currently being used in federal Head Start, Even Start, Reading First and Early Reading First programs

2. why Is the PPVT Test?

- The language processes that underlie the ability to comprehend written text are the same as those that underlie the ability to comprehend speech

3. What is the principle of the PPVT Test?

- Children make sense of the words they see by comparing them to the words they have heard

4. What does research tell us?

- Most vocabulary is learned indirectly

- Some vocabulary must be taught directly

-Poor vocabulary is a hallmark of language, literacy, and cognitive difficulties

5. Basals and Ceilings

- Basal Set - The lowest set of items administered containing 1 or 0 errors

- Ceiling Set - The highest set of items administered containing 8 or more errors

6. Step to begin testing:

- Find quiet spot for testing sessions

- Need 2 chairs and flat table

- Have materials ready (test easel A or B with corresponding record form)

- Sit in "L" shape - corner of the table

- Establish rapport (relaxed atmosphere, "I have some pictures to show you...")

- Fill in child's demographic information on the front page of record form

- Calculate Chronological Age (CA) to get your starting point

7. How to find the chronological age and subsequently where the test taker should start?

- Line up the year, month, day in a equation

- 2015 8 14 (Evaluation Date)

- 2012 2 5 (Date of Birth)

- Subtract from left to right, borrow from left to right, add 12 to the top month, add 30 to the top day

- years 6 months 9 days
